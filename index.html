We are driven by the vision of an equitable future driven by innovation, owned by patients, and empowered by the healthcare community.
CodeX is the only member-driven community focused on solving the problem of data silos endemic to our current health care system for specialty data. Dedicated to radical improvements in patient outcomes and supercharging the capability of care team and researchers.

CodeX is a global community of healthcare stakeholders currently working on data standards in three domains.

Join Us and Spread the Word

Cancer

The first domain that the CodeX community tackled is cancer. Using the minimal Common Oncology Data Elements (mCODE), an open data standard enabling enabled high-quality data capture for cancer that can be widely adopted.

70+ oncology definitions in use at Dana Farber, MD Anderson, Mass General Hospital and 44 other institutions. Versions of the mCODE and associated modeling tools are currently operational in over 250 organizations.

NEW DOMAIN

Advance the standardization, interoperability and exchange of genomic data to improve care processes, clinical workflows, and patient outcomes from all areas of clinical care

Cardiovascular Health

Build and Innovate on Our Work

Data is unreliable, unstandardized, lacks integrity and is largely unusable. Data intake questions and answers are based on the localized priorities, methods and judgments of individual physicians and providers with each representing their own mini silo within today’s Digital Health Ecosystem. Today essential patient response data is often captured in unstructured text, such as clinical notes, PDF files, or other non-standardized reports creating critical gaps in the patient story and making any analysis to determine treatment options entirely manual.

iCAREdata

Clinical trials are the gold standard for clinical care interventions. 92% of cancer patients in the United States are not enrolled in clinical trials due to poor data collection of their cancer therapies. This project focuses on collecting key outcome data that is standardized, research-grade, and longitudinal across organizations to promote health equity and reduce burden on health sites.
